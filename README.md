## 📝 Overview
This is the second project of the **Data Analysis with Python** certification from [freeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/). The primary objective is to use the Pandas library to analyze, filter, and extract meaningful statistics from a demographic dataset based on the 1994 US Census database.

## 🎯 Key Insights & Analysis
The Python script is designed to process the data and output answers to several specific demographic questions:
* **Demographics:** Count of people by race and the average age of men.
* **Education:** The percentage of people possessing a Bachelor's degree.
* **Income vs. Education:** The percentage of people with advanced education (Bachelors, Masters, or Doctorate) who make more than `>50K`, compared to those without advanced education.
* **Work Hours:** The minimum number of hours worked per week and the percentage of those working minimum hours who still earn `>50K`.
* **Global Economics:** Identifying the country with the highest percentage of people earning `>50K` and calculating that percentage.
* **Specific Demographics:** Determining the most popular occupation for high earners (`>50K`) in India.

## 🗄️ The Dataset
The dataset (`adult.data.csv`) is sourced from the **UCI Machine Learning Repository**. It contains robust categorical and numerical data, including features like `age`, `workclass`, `education`, `marital-status`, `occupation`, `race`, `sex`, `native-country`, and `salary`.
